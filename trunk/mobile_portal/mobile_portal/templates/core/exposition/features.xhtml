Contact search

Search the University e-mail and telephone directories from your phone.

Also included are important contact numbers for students, including welfare, emergency and University services.

Maps

Search for colleges, departments, restaurants, bus stops and much more.

Each page provides a range of useful information, including:

  • live arrival predictions for bus stops
  • opening hours and phone numbers for pubs, restaurants and cafés
  • collection times for post boxes
  • images for colleges

Library search

Search for books and other items by author, title or ISBN. Results are displayed with the nearest first and simple maps to guide you to the library.

Podcasts

Listen to any podcast published through the University's podcast service and Apple's iTunes U.

Search

Search is heavily integrated into Mobile Oxford.

You can search for any text on the site, with results presented with relevant metadata and an icon showing which part of the site they came from.

In addition you can also go direct to the page you want in the following by entering…

  • a post code to find items in that area
  • an eight-digit bus stop code (e.g. 69312345) to go straight to that stop
  • a central stop code (e.g. C1) to go to that bus stop
  • an OUCS unit code (e.g. ashmus) to view a unit's page
  • a ten or 13 digit ISBN to view a library record
  • an eight or 13 digit ISSN to view a serial library holding

Other features

On top of all this, Mobile Oxford provides easy access to the following sources of information:

Results release announcements
The Examination Schools now publish all results through Student Self-Service, announcing their availability on the Schools website. Mobile Oxford saves you worrying about whether your results are available while you're on the move.
Weather
Local observations and forecasts for Oxford are presented in a concise form.
Webcams
View webcams from accross the city. Useful for gauging traffic or weather.
OUCS status
Easy access to the availability of key OUCS services.
